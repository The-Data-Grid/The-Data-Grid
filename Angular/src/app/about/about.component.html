What is The Data Grid?

The Data Grid is an evolving open source software distribution that helps communities monitor and improve their resource usage. It is composed of a web application and mobile data collection app for in-field data collection.

What is the purpose of The Data Grid?

To get an accurate and detailed picture of resource usage within our communities. Building an accurate and detailed picture of the collective impact consumptive activities make is the first step in recognizing areas in need of improvement and formulating comprehensive solutions to address them. When forming recommendations, we examine the intersection between financial, environmental, and social metrics.

History

In 2019 students at UCLA started compiling utility audit data from various organizations on campus to get a big-picture view of campus sustainability and macro-trends. They realized many independent organizations had developed diverse and novel methods of studying usage but detailed documentation and standardization was scarce. Knowledge was being lost or stored away. When data was available, the variety of storages made them hard to compare. As they continued compiling, they saw the potential for a centralized system to provide coordination and support for these diverse groups while maintaining their independence and unique strengths.
